The Story

What if I told you that this trendy shopping complex was once the site of a massive industrial bakery that fed half of Auckland?

"I remember when my grandmother would come here in the 1950s to buy bread that was still warm from the ovens. The whole street smelled like fresh baking every morning." - Local resident Margaret Thompson, whose family has lived in Ponsonby for four generations.

Fascinating Facts
  • The original Griffin's Biscuit Factory operated here from 1908 to 1992, producing over 50 million biscuits annually at its peak.
  • During World War II, this facility was converted to produce hardtack biscuits for New Zealand troops overseas.
  • The distinctive red brick was imported from England in the early 1900s as ballast in ships, then repurposed for construction.

The Story

Beneath your feet lies the remains of Auckland's first public cemetery, where over 3,000 people were laid to rest before the park was created.

"My great-great-grandfather helped move the headstones when they converted this from cemetery to park in the 1880s. Fascinating Facts
  • Western Park was Auckland's first public cemetery from 1842 to 1886, with over 3,000 burials before it was converted to parkland.
  • The large pohutukawa trees were planted in 1890 and are now over 130 years old, making them some of Auckland's oldest urban trees.
  • During World War II, the park was used as a victory garden where locals grew vegetables to support the war effort.
